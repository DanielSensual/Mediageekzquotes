Creative Direction

Cinematic, not corporate.
Prestigious, not generic.

Your event coverage should feel like a luxury brand campaign — every shot intentional, every frame elevated. We don't make "recap videos." We create brand cinema.

✦ The Prestige Standard

  • Cinematic camera movement — gimbals, sliders, dolly energy
  • Dramatic lighting awareness — stage spots, natural window light, golden hour
  • Editorial framing — shallow DOF, negative space, intentional composition
  • Warm, rich color grading — not flat corporate gray
  • Sound design that builds atmosphere — not just background music
  • Human moments — genuine reactions, handshakes, laughter

✕ What We Avoid

  • Static tripod talking heads with fluorescent lighting
  • "Corporate training video" energy and pacing
  • Generic stock music and title cards
  • Flat, overlit footage with no contrast or mood
  • Shaky handheld without purpose
  • Over-produced motion graphics that feel templated
Color Science

The Prestige Grade

Rich, warm editorial cinema. Deep shadows with champagne highlights and protected skin tones. The grade should feel premium and timeless — never clinical, never trendy.

Deep Midnight #0A0E1A
Slate Navy #1E293B
Steel #334155
Prestige Gold #D4A843
Champagne #F0E6D3
Ivory #F8FAFC

Shadows

Deep navy-black with subtle warmth. Never crushed, never muddy. Lifted blacks with teal undertones for cinematic depth. Low contrast S-curve for editorial feel.

Skin Tones

Protected and warm — golden olive base, never orange or pink. Pull global saturation 10-15%, then selectively boost skin warmth. Skin is the most important element on stage.

Highlights

Champagne roll-off — warm, never blown. Stage lights get a halation glow effect. Kodak 5219 film emulation at 25-35% as the foundation layer for texture.

Stage Lighting

Respect the venue's lighting design — use it, don't fight it. Spot-meter for speaker skin, let backgrounds fall naturally. Mixed color temps are a feature, not a problem.
